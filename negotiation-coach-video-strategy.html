MODE: Prep I have a performance review coming up next week with my manager. I've been at the company for 2 years, I was promoted 8 months ago but my salary wasn't adjusted to match the new role. Market rate for my position is $95-110K and I'm currently at $82K. My manager is generally supportive but the company has been tightening budgets. I have a competing offer from another company at $105K but I'd prefer to stay. Give me a complete prep strategy with: 1. Specific opening language to use 2. How to handle the likely pushback ("budgets are tight") 3. How to use the competing offer without making it feel like an ultimatum 4. What NOT to say (anti-patterns to avoid) 5. A step-by-step sequence for the conversation

I wanted to talk about my compensation — specifically the gap between my current salary and what the market data shows for this role."
