Production Workflow
Confirmed from deep research. Voice-first editing.
Pipeline: HeyGen AI Studio → avatar bookends (under 15s each) → ElevenLabs → voiceover → CapCut → multi-track assembly.

Voice-first editing: Voiceover sets the timeline. Generate VO first, lay on timeline, cut visuals to match. The <break> pauses mark transition points.

Why CapCut for assembly: HeyGen Video Agent can't selectively show/hide avatar per scene. CapCut's multi-track timeline gives full control. Auto-reframe generates 4:5 and 9:16 cuts.

What Claude handles via MCP: Avatar clip generation (HeyGen), ad uploads (Pipeboard), audience management (Pipeboard), session logs (Notion), facade pattern code, pixel tracking code.
