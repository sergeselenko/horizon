Four Proven Frameworks
Every recommendation traces back to a specific principle from one of these systems.
Jim Camp
Start with No
Decision-based negotiation. Mission-setting, emotional discipline, structured agendas, and the discipline to never need the deal.
Chris Voss
Never Split the Difference
Tactical empathy. Emotional labeling, calibrated questions, accusation audits, mirroring, and getting to "that's right."
Fisher & Ury
Getting to Yes
Interest-based negotiation. BATNA, ZOPA, separating people from problems, and inventing options for mutual gain.
Robert Cialdini
Influence
The psychology of persuasion. Reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, and scarcity — used ethically.
Anti-Pattern Detection
The coach actively watches for and flags these common negotiation mistakes.
🚨 Neediness Signals
Language that telegraphs desperation — excessive follow-ups, offering concessions before being asked, over-explaining why the deal is good for you.
⚖️ Compromise Traps
Splitting the difference, premature concessions, accepting vague commitments without specifics.
💧 Emotional Leakage
Showing too much excitement, reacting defensively to pushback, arguing to be right instead of asking questions.
🎯 Yes-Seeking
Questions designed to force agreement, momentum-selling tactics, closed questions that invite one-word answers.
Who This Is For

This coaching system is for anyone who negotiates and wants to be better at it — whether that's a salary conversation, a vendor contract, a business partnership, a real estate deal, or a difficult client situation. Your First Session

Open your Negotiation Coach project, start a new chat, go to the Scenarios tab on this page, and pick the scenario closest to your situation. Fill in the fields, copy the completed prompt, and paste it into the chat.

"I'm preparing to negotiate my compensation for a Senior Product Manager role. They offered $145K base with a $15K signing bonus. I know market rate is $155–170K for this role in my city. I have another offer at $150K but like this company more. I'm worried about pushing too hard because the hiring manager mentioned budget constraints. Help me prepare."

The coach will analyze your dual-offer leverage, calculate the employer's likely flexibility range, build an opening strategy, and give you the specific words to use.

Tips for Best Results
Be specific. "$145K offer" is much better than "they made an offer." Real numbers and real context unlock real strategy.
Use real situations. The coach is most useful when you bring something you're actually facing — not hypotheticals.
Start a new chat each time. Your project settings carry over, but conversation history doesn't. Fresh chat = clean context.
Try different modes. Prep for fast advice, Strategy for deep analysis, Draft for emails, Role-Play for practice, Debrief for post-game.
Privacy note. Use role descriptions instead of names if privacy matters (e.g., "the hiring manager" instead of a real name).

Troubleshooting

Responses seem generic — what's wrong?

Two common causes: (1) Custom Instructions didn't paste correctly — go to your project settings and verify the full text is in the Custom Instructions field. (2) You're chatting outside the project — make sure you start chats from within your Negotiation Coach project, not from Claude's main screen.

Do I need Claude Pro?

Recommended but not required. Pro gives longer conversations for complex negotiations. Free works for straightforward situations.

Can I use this with ChatGPT?

No. This kit is built specifically for Claude Projects. Loading these files into other tools won't produce the same results.

Can I customize the instructions?

Yes. Advanced users can edit Custom Instructions. But try it as-is first — it's designed to work out of the box.

Is my data private?

Your conversations are between you and Claude, subject to Anthropic's privacy policy. No third-party service, no external database, no tracking.
