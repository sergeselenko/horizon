Four proven frameworks

Every recommendation traces back to a specific principle.

📘

Jim Camp — Start with No

Decision-based negotiation. Mission-setting, emotional discipline, structured agendas, and the discipline to never need the deal.

📗

Chris Voss — Never Split the Difference

Tactical empathy. Emotional labeling, calibrated questions, accusation audits, mirroring, and getting to "that's right."

📙

Fisher & Ury — Getting to Yes

Interest-based negotiation. BATNA, ZOPA, separating people from problems, and inventing options for mutual gain.

📕

Robert Cialdini — Influence

The psychology of persuasion. Reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, and scarcity — used ethically.

Anti-pattern detection

The coach actively watches for and flags these mistakes.

🚨

Neediness signals

Language that telegraphs desperation — excessive follow-ups, offering concessions before being asked.

⚖️

Compromise traps

Splitting the difference, premature concessions, accepting vague commitments without specifics.

💧

Emotional leakage

Showing too much excitement, reacting defensively to pushback, arguing to be right instead of asking questions.

🎯

Yes-seeking

Questions designed to force agreement, momentum-selling tactics, closed questions that invite one-word answers.

Who this is for

This coaching system is for anyone who negotiates and wants to be better at it — whether that's a salary conversation, a vendor contract, a business partnership, a real estate deal, or a difficult client situation.

You don't need negotiation experience. You don't need to have read the books. The coach has the frameworks built in — you bring your situation, and it gives you specific strategy, specific language, and specific practice.

Role-Play Practice

You can't role-play with a book. The coach plays your counterparty, pushes back with realistic tactics, then gives you an honest debrief.

Each role-play puts you in a live back-and-forth negotiation. The coach stays in character — using real pressure tactics, not softball questions. When you type "debrief," the coach drops character and tells you exactly what worked, what didn't, and what to try differently.

🎯
The Hardball Buyer — Anchors 30-40% below your price, manufactures deadlines, claims cheaper alternatives.
😢
The Emotional Manipulator — Uses guilt, loyalty, and personal appeals. Subtle enough that it's hard to push back.
🤐
The Silent Treatment — You sent your proposal and... nothing. Practice following up without chasing.
🍕
The Nibbler — "Oh, one more thing..." — small asks that add up fast after the deal is done.
👥
Good Cop / Bad Cop — One friendly, one harsh. A coordinated act most people fall for.

3 Guided Tools

These aren't scenarios — they're step-by-step walkthroughs that apply specific negotiation techniques to your real numbers and situations.

📊

Ackerman Guided Walkthrough

Enter your target price. The tool calculates your opening offer at 65%, maps the three raises to 85%, 95%, and 100%, and coaches you on exactly what to say at each stage — including empathy phrases, calibrated questions, and when to deploy a non-monetary sweetener.

⏳

Follow-Up After Silence

Builds a timed 4-message sequence: Day 3 uses a no-oriented question, Day 7 adds new value, Day 14 deploys the "have you given up on this?" technique, and Day 21+ is a graceful close that leaves the door open. Each message uses a different approach — never just "checking in."

🛡️

Accusation Audit Builder

List everything negative the other person might be thinking about you — then say it out loud before they do. The tool walks you through brainstorming what you're missing, sequencing from least to most severe, and coaching you on delivery and tone.
